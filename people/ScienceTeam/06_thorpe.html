Andrew Thorpe

Project Scientist
JPL

twitter Google Scholar ResearchID OrcidID

Andrew Thorpe is a technologist in the JPL imaging spectroscopy group with a decade of experience working with imaging spectrometer data. He has developed quantitative gas retrievals for AVIRIS, AVIRIS-NG, and EMIT using differential optical absorption spectroscopy, methods to estimate emission rates, and contributed to projects aimed at visualizing methane results and reducing data latency. Andrew is currently an EMIT Science Team member leading the EMIT greenhouse gas mapping application, and the Deputy Instrument Scientist for Carbon Plume Mapper.

Education

  • Ph.D., Geography | Univ. of California, Santa Barbaray | 2015
  • B.S., Geology | Brown University | 2004

Professional Experience

Andrew Thorpe has extensive experience in high-resolution methane mapping, ranging from retrieval algorithms (A. Thorpe et al., 2014; Andrew K. Thorpe et al., 2017; Andrew K. Thorpe et al., 2021) and the first ever controlled release experiment involving aircraft and spectroscopic mapping (Andrew K. Thorpe, Frankenberg, Aubrey, et al., 2016) to data analysis and sectorial up-scaling of methane fluxes (Daniel H. Cusworth et al., 2022; Duren et al., 2019; Andrew K. Thorpe et al., 2020; Andrew K. Thorpe, Green, et al., 2023).
